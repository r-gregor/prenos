1 - withe echo -en:
for i in $(seq 0 100); do echo -en "\033[200D$i %" && sleep 0.0125; done; echo -e "\n"
output: 1 - 9, 10 - 100 % (in same place)

2 - with printf:
for i in $(seq 0 100); do printf "\033[200D%02d %%" "$i" && sleep 0.05; done; echo -e "\n"
output: 01 - 09, 10 - 100 % (in same place)


for i in $(seq 0 100); do printf "\033[200D%03d %%" "$i" && sleep 0.05; done; echo -e "\n"
output: 001 - 009, 010 - 100 % (in same place)

Explanation:
"\033[200D" acts like \r
